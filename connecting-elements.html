Connecting Elements – The Nobel Prize in Chemistry

Connecting Elements is a museum exhibition that displays the work of Nobel Laureates in Chemistry.
Through interactive themes, visitors learn how elements and molecules play a role in creating the world around us, and how chemical reactions can help to consciously shape the future in our planet.

See more at ATELIER BRÜCKNER

My role
UI Design - Museum Graphics

Year
2018

Location
Dubai

Background

The Nobel Prize Museum believes that ideas can change the world. To make these ideas tangible, they create traveling exhibitions to honor the courage, creativity and perseverance of the Nobel Laureates, and help to inspire audiences worldwide.
In 2018, the award-winnging architecture studio ATELIER BRÜCKNER was comisioned to design an interactive experience about Chemistry in Dubai.
I supported the project as an intern in UI and Exhibition Museum.

Goals

  • Showcase complex information in several languages.
  • Display comprehensively, the live and contribution of Nobel Laureates.
  • Generate engagement, curiosity and wonder.
  • Work iternationally with teams in based in Germany, Denmark and The Arab Emirates.

The team

  • 2 Project Managers
  • 5 Designers
  • 4 Interior Architects

The process

  1. Concept
  2. Design
  3. Tender
  4. Handoff
  5. Installation

My key contributions

I focused on crafting user interfaces that would help visitors to interact with the elements of periodic table, and discover the achievements of Nobel Laureates. 
Aditionally I created large-scale infographics that enhanced the exhibition experience.
During the creative process I designed wireframes, storyboards, icons, prototypes, brand guidelines, as well the handover of tender assets for development and print production.

Challenges

The exhibition design was planned in Stuttgart, developed in Stockolm and built in Dubai. This difference in locations, made especially challenging the design, handoff and implementation of the interactive user interfaces.
All content for the exhibiton was written an English and Arabic. Since both languages have completely different syntax and grammatic, it was not easy to find layout systems for the text.

Outcomes

Connecting Elements was devided five different thematic areas: Elements, Molecules & Structures, Chemical Reactions, Chemistry of Life, Chemistry Changes the World.
Manifold hands-on- and media stations are intended to engage the visitor in taking action to research and explore. The visitors discover four different thematic areas and learn how chemistry influences the environment and our everyday life. Main target group are children and adolescents 12 years and older.

Learnings

Storytelling is King – A cohesive story must be clearly established from start, in order to follow a path to communicate information, and connect with the audience.

Teamwork makes the Dreamwork – The collaboration between teams in different time-zones was challenging but a good strategy, communication and commitment made this project, one of the most meaningful experiences in my professional journey.
