Verimi – Your digital ID. Safe and easy  to use everywhere.

Verimi develops solutions for the digital identity management of the future.

My work focuses on delivering a multi-channel brand experience for mobile and web.

Discover more at verimi.de

Year
2020

Location
Berlin

My role
UX/UI - Brand Strategy

Problem to solve

Enables users to safely store and re-use personal data, through an in-line flow between Verimi and Identity TM.

Management goals

  1. Identify in which steps of the workflow are users dropping off and determine the most opportunity for impact.
  2. Increase user base to 25% in Q1 and Q2 2021.
  3. Build up a Product that will attract 2 more partners and clients in Q1 and Q2 2021.

The team

  • 1 Product Manager
  • 1 UX/UI Designer (me)
  • 1 Frontend Developer
  • 2 Backend Developers

The Brief

  1. Design an intuitive user experience, keeping in mind user needs, business goals and technical constraints.
  2. Facilitate workshops between Product, Marketing and Management to align on the concept and the content.
  3. Provide user journeys, wireframes (for mobile and web), mock-ups and clickable prototypes.
  4. Work closely with software engineers in the implementation of the user flows.

The process

My key contributions

During the research phase, I gathered valuable information about the behaviour of our components. I found out that the components used to create a password – the input field and the requirements – were ambiguous and therefore, users were dropping off.

I tested different alternatives with internal and external stakeholders and finally came up with a seamless, clear, and not-intrusive proposal.

Later on, this new proposal was positively received by other product managers, and it was added to our Design System.

To increase our user base, I focused on making the flow more engaging through several micro-animations that would give positive reinforcement in real-time to the users. The result was a successful increase of 5% in our user-base on the first two weeks after launch.

This project became essential to Verimi, because it showcases an example of multiple services, and it can be used to onboard potential stakeholders. Currently, there are conversations with other German identity providers to integrate the solution I helped to design.

Outcomes

Connecting Elements was devided five different thematic areas: Elements, Molecules & Structures, Chemical Reactions, Chemistry of Life, Chemistry Changes the World.
Manifold hands-on- and media stations are intended to engage the visitor in taking action to research and explore. The visitors discover four different thematic areas and learn how chemistry influences the environment and our everyday life. Main target group are children and adolescents 12 years and older.

Learnings

Storytelling is King

A cohesive story must be clearly established from start, in order to follow a path to communicate information, and connect with the audience.

Teamwork makes the Dreamwork – The collaboration between teams in different time-zones was challenging but a good strategy, communication and commitment made this project, one of the most meaningful experiences in my professional journey.
